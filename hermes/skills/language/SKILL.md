# Language

French language help — phrases, translations, pronunciation, and contextual vocab.

## Trigger
- "how do I say", "translate", "what does X mean", "French for", "pronunciation"

## Format
Always include:
- French phrase
- Pronunciation in parentheses (phonetic English approximation)
- English meaning
- Context for when to use it

Example: "Ou sont les toilettes?" (oo sohn lay twah-LET) — "Where are the bathrooms?"

## Essential Phrases by Context

### Greetings (use these every interaction)
- Bonjour (bohn-ZHOOR) — Hello / Good day
- Bonsoir (bohn-SWAHR) — Good evening
- Au revoir (oh reh-VWAHR) — Goodbye
- Merci (mehr-SEE) — Thank you
- S'il vous plait (seel voo PLAY) — Please
- Excusez-moi (ex-koo-zay MWAH) — Excuse me

### Ordering Food
- Je voudrais... (zhuh voo-DRAY) — I would like...
- L'addition, s'il vous plait — The check, please
- Une carafe d'eau (oon kah-RAHF doh) — A pitcher of tap water
- Un cafe (uhn kah-FAY) — An espresso (not drip coffee)
- Un cafe allonge (ah-lohn-ZHAY) — Closer to American coffee

### With Kids
- Est-ce que vous avez une chaise haute? (es kuh voo zah-VAY oon shez OHT) — Do you have a high chair?
- Ou est l'aire de jeux? (oo ay LAIR duh zhuh) — Where is the playground?
- Mon enfant a trois ans (mohn ahn-FAHN ah twah zahn) — My child is three years old

### Getting Help
- Parlez-vous anglais? (par-LAY voo ahn-GLAY) — Do you speak English?
- Je ne comprends pas (zhuh nuh kohm-PRAHN pah) — I don't understand
- Pouvez-vous m'aider? (poo-VAY voo may-DAY) — Can you help me?

### Shopping
- Combien ca coute? (kohm-BYEHN sah KOOT) — How much does it cost?
- Je regarde seulement (zhuh reh-GARD suhl-MAHN) — I'm just looking

## Rules
- Keep pronunciation guides simple — English-speaker approximations, not IPA
- When they ask how to say something, also include the polite version
- If they share a phrase they heard, explain what it means and when locals use it
- Build a running vocabulary in memory based on what they ask about most
