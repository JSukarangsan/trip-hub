# Transport

Directions, transit help, and getting-around logistics.

- "how do we get to", "directions", "metro", "bus", "RER", "taxi", "Uber", "walk", "how far"

## Context
- Read `context/trip.md` for current apartment address (date-dependent)
- Paris apartment: Ukio, Boulevard de Grenelle, 15e — Bir-Hakeim (L6), Dupleix (L6)
- Provence: car-based (Bastide du Mourre, then Domaine de Chalamon)

## Capabilities

### Directions from Apartment
- Always give directions FROM the current apartment (based on today's date)
- Include: walking time, metro option (line + stops + transfers), which is better with stroller
- For destinations under 20 min walk in good weather, suggest walking first

### Metro / RER
- Paris metro basics: Navigo pass (weekly, zones 1-5), tap in/out
- Sloane is under 4 = rides free, no ticket needed
- RER for airport (CDG = RER B) and day trips (Versailles = RER C)
- Report disruptions if known (search web for "RATP perturbations" if asked)

### Taxi / Uber
- Uber works in Paris, generally EUR 10-25 within central Paris
- For airport: taxi flat rate CDG ↔ Paris = EUR 55 (Right Bank) / EUR 62 (Left Bank)
- G7 taxi app is the French alternative to Uber
- With car seat: Uber has car seat option, or bring portable one

### Provence Transport
- When in Provence (Aug 21-26): car rental likely needed
- Distances between villages, drive times
- Parking tips for Provence towns

### Stroller Considerations
- Metro: not all stations have elevators. Lines 1 and 14 are fully accessible.
- Bir-Hakeim (L6) has stairs — suggest Dupleix or bus alternatives when stroller is heavy
- Bus is often easier than metro with a stroller

## Rules
- Always mention stroller accessibility for metro directions
- Default to the simplest route, not the fastest (fewer transfers > 3 min savings)
- For evening outings, mention if taxi/Uber is the better call (tired kid, late night)
