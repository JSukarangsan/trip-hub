# Shopping

Grocery stores, pharmacies, and where to buy specific items.

## Trigger
- "where to buy", "grocery", "supermarket", "pharmacy", "diapers", "sunscreen", "SIM card", "store"

## Context
- Read `context/trip.md` for current apartment location
- Paris 15e apartment is near Bir-Hakeim / Dupleix metro

## Capabilities

### Grocery Stores Near Apartment (15e)
- Search for nearest Monoprix, Franprix, Carrefour City, Picard (frozen foods)
- Opening hours (many open until 9-10pm, some open Sunday morning)
- What to stock on arrival: water, milk, snacks, fruit, bread basics

### Pharmacies
- Nearest pharmacie to apartment (green cross sign)
- 24-hour pharmacy: Pharmacie des Champs-Elysees (84 Avenue des Champs-Elysees)
- Key items with French names:
  - Diapers = Couches (Pampers brand available)
  - Sunscreen = Creme solaire (La Roche-Posay, Avene are French brands, excellent quality)
  - Children's Tylenol = Doliprane (paracetamol, OTC)
  - Ibuprofen = Advil or Nurofen
  - Band-aids = Pansements
  - Bug spray = Anti-moustiques

### Specific Items
- SIM card: Free Mobile (free.fr) has affordable tourist plans. Available at Free stores or tabac shops.
- Baby supplies: Monoprix has good baby section. For specialty items, try Aubert or Bebe 9.
- Wine: Nicolas (chain, reliable), or local cavistes for better selection
- Adapters/electronics: Darty or FNAC stores
- Clothing: If needed, Monoprix has decent basics

### Provence Shopping
- When in Provence (Aug 21-26): village markets, local producers
- Smaller grocery options — Carrefour Contact, Casino, Intermarche
- Stock up before arriving — rural areas have limited hours

## Rules
- Always check if it's Sunday or a holiday before recommending a store
- For urgent needs (medicine, diapers at night), know the nearest late-night/24hr options
- French equivalents matter — help them find what they need even if brands are different
