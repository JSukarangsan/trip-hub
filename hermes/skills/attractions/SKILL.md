## Capabilities

### Museums & Cultural Sites
- Eiffel Tower: advance booking required, kid lines available, stroller-friendly
- Louvre: book timed entry, kid-friendly wings (Egyptian, sculpture garden), free under 18
- Musee d'Orsay: impressionists, book ahead, manageable size with toddler
- Musee de l'Orangerie: Monet water lilies, small and toddler-friendly (quick visit)
- Cite des Sciences: interactive kids section (Cite des Enfants for ages 2-7)
- Jardin d'Acclimatation: amusement park + zoo in Bois de Boulogne

### Parks & Outdoor
- Jardin du Luxembourg: puppet shows, toy sailboats, playground (paid, ~EUR 2)
- Champ de Mars: right near the apartment, open space for Sloane to run
- Tuileries Garden: carousel, trampolines in summer
- Bois de Boulogne: rowboats, Jardin d'Acclimatation
- Canal Saint-Martin: walk along the locks, cafe-lined

### Day Trips from Paris
- Versailles: RER C, ~45 min. Book ahead. Gardens free except fountain show days.
- Giverny (Monet's garden): ~75 min by train. Best Apr–Oct.
- Fontainebleau: ~1 hr train. Less crowded than Versailles. Forest walks.
- Chantilly: ~25 min train. Castle + horse museum + cream.

### Provence Activities (Aug 21–26)
- Luberon village hopping: Gordes, Roussillon (ochre trail), Menerbes, Bonnieux
- Saint-Remy-de-Provence: market days, Van Gogh trail, Roman ruins (Glanum)
- Lavender fields (late season but some still blooming)
- Wine tastings with kid-friendly estates (outdoor areas)

## Rules
- Always check if advance booking is needed (Eiffel, Louvre, Orsay fill up in summer)
- Suggest morning visits for popular sites (less crowded, cooler, pre-nap energy)
- Group activities during friends' visit (Jul 25–Aug 2) — 5+ adults + kids
- Rainy day alternatives always ready
- Don't suggest the same thing twice unless they want to revisit
