# Local Calendar — Paris 15e / Nearby

## Weekly Markets (Marchés)

### Marché Grenelle (home market)
- **Days**: Wednesday & Sunday, 7am–2:30pm
- **Location**: Boulevard de Grenelle, between Dupleix and La Motte-Picquet
- **Distance**: 2 min walk from apartment
- **Notes**: Large open-air market. Produce, cheese, rotisserie chicken, flowers, clothes. Best produce vendors on the Dupleix end. Get there before 11am for best selection.

### Marché Président Wilson
- **Days**: Wednesday & Saturday, 7am–2:30pm
- **Location**: Avenue du Président Wilson, 16e (between Iéna and Alma-Marceau)
- **Distance**: ~15 min by L6 from Bir-Hakeim to Trocadéro + walk
- **Notes**: Upscale market. Great cheese, charcuterie, and prepared foods. More expensive than Grenelle but higher quality.

### Marché Saxe-Breteuil
- **Days**: Thursday & Saturday, 7am–2:30pm
- **Location**: Avenue de Saxe, 7e (Eiffel Tower views while shopping)
- **Distance**: ~10 min walk or 1 stop on L6
- **Notes**: Photogenic market with Eiffel Tower backdrop. Good produce, flowers, and crêpe stand.

### Marché Raspail
- **Days**: Tuesday & Friday (regular), Sunday (bio/organic)
- **Location**: Boulevard Raspail, 6e
- **Distance**: ~20 min by metro
- **Notes**: Sunday organic market is famous. Worth the trip once. Pricey but excellent quality.

### Marché d'Aligre
- **Days**: Tuesday–Sunday, 7:30am–1:30pm (covered hall open to 7:30pm)
- **Location**: Place d'Aligre, 12e
- **Distance**: ~25 min by metro
- **Notes**: Most local/authentic Paris market. Covered hall (Beauvau) has permanent vendors. Cheapest produce in Paris. Great North African food stalls.

## Recurring Local Events (July–August)

### Paris Plages
- **When**: Mid-July through mid-August
- **Where**: Along the Seine (Right Bank) and Bassin de la Villette
- **What**: Temporary beaches, sand, palm trees, activities. Free. Kid-friendly zones with sprinklers and play areas.
- **Nearest**: Seine section accessible from Bir-Hakeim area

### Cinéma en Plein Air (Outdoor Cinema)
- **When**: Mid-July through August, screenings at sundown (~9:30pm)
- **Where**: Parc de la Villette, 19e
- **What**: Free outdoor film screenings. Bring a blanket. Films in French (some with subtitles).
- **Notes**: Late for Sloane but possible on special evenings

## Brocantes & Vide-Greniers (Flea Markets)
- Pop up throughout the summer in various arrondissements
- Check vide-greniers.org for weekly listings
- Good for: vintage finds, kids' clothes, books, small gifts
